Transparency & responsibility

Your help arrives.

Trust is an obligation: to ensure that funds actually reach those in need.

First, our heartfelt thanks for your donation. Without the many donors here in Germany, we could not fund any projects in health and education – nor could we offer school scholarships.

And not least, we pass on the thanks of our partners in Africa – Sr. Basilisa Panga, Fr. Valentine Karama and Bishop Antony.

Thanks to our main sponsors

  • "Aktion Menschen in Not" of the Bietigheimer Zeitung newspaper, which contributes generously each year.
  • The City of Bietigheim-Bissingen, which has supported our school projects for many years.
  • The parishes of Pleidelsheim and Ingersheim, who have backed our health projects for many years.
And above all, thanks for the trust placed in us – to channel donations undiminished to those in need.

How we ensure your donation arrives

  • Application required. Funds are released only after we receive an application detailing the expected benefit, the costs, and how the project will be implemented.
  • Closing reports with receipts. Project closing reports with invoiced costs and photographs are mandatory.
  • Site visits every 2–3 years. We travel to Tanzania to verify implementation in person and to talk with the people involved.
    By the way: flight costs are normally paid by the travellers themselves.
  • Written rules in the Student Support Program. Selection criteria and reporting requirements are documented. Lists of pupils with class levels and disbursed amounts are submitted every six months. On site visits we routinely meet sponsored students to verify compliance.

Trust is earned.

Every report, every receipt, every visit. For 23 years.
